How It Works

Three things the app does to help your family solve selective eating

1. Identify Patterns

Log what your child explores. The app analyzes textures, flavors, temperatures, and colors they gravitate toward. You see which sensory patterns are holding them back.

2. Bridge Foods

The app finds bridge foods: foods they already enjoy, plus one new sensory element. If they like crunchy salty crackers, try crunchy salty pretzels. Every suggestion is doable.

3. Celebrate Progress

Your child earns badges and streaks. You see the data. Mealtimes shift from battles to celebrating how brave they're being. The guilt is gone.

How the Feedback Loop Works

Every time your child explores a food, you log it together in the app. Did they just look? Touch? Smell? Taste? These tiny steps matter. They show us what their nervous system is ready for next.

As your child logs more foods, patterns emerge. You see what textures they gravitate toward. What temperatures feel safe. What flavors they're curious about. The app learns their unique sensory profile.

Then it recommends bridge foods. Familiar foods plus one new sensory element. Crunchy like crackers, but salty like pretzels. Foods that have the highest chance of success. Science-backed. Personalized to them. Not generic lists.

You track progress. They unlock planets and badges. Together, you're solving this one small sensory step at a time.

💡 Parent Education Built In

The SOS Approach teaches us that your role is to support, not push. Sensory Galaxy includes guidance on:

  • Recognizing when your child needs a break - Signs of sensory overload or stress
  • When to pause - Comfort first, always. Progress can wait.
  • Supporting problem-solving, not forcing - Help them explore at their pace, in their way
  • Building confidence - Celebrate every phase (looking counts, touching counts, tasting counts)
