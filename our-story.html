How Sensory Galaxy Was Born

A parent's journey with celiac disease, selective eating, and finding hope through the SOS Approach.

The Beginning: When Everything Changed

When Mackenzie was just one year old, she was diagnosed with celiac disease. For our family, that moment felt like the start of a long, complicated journey. We learned what she couldn't eat. We learned to read labels obsessively. We learned to cook in ways we never expected to have to.

But there was something else we didn't expect: how much her relationship with food would become a source of anxiety—not just for her, but for all of us.

As Mackenzie grew, mealtimes became increasingly difficult. The combination of celiac restrictions and her own sensory sensitivities meant that her "safe foods" list was incredibly short. We watched her become visibly nervous around new foods. Family dinners that should have been joyful felt tense. We found ourselves wondering: Was it the disease? Was it our parenting? Was it something we were doing wrong?

The Turning Point: Discovering SOS

Everything shifted when we learned about the Sequential Oral Sensory (SOS) Approach to Feeding. For the first time, someone explained what was actually happening: Mackenzie's nervous system wasn't wired to process new foods the way other kids' were. This wasn't stubbornness. This wasn't failure. This was sensory sensitivity—and it was real.

The SOS Approach removed the pressure entirely. Instead of "you need to eat this," it became "let's explore this together, at your pace." LOOK. TOUCH. SMELL. LICK. TASTE. Five phases. No rush. No judgment.

The transformation was almost immediate. Within weeks, Mackenzie stopped being afraid of new foods. She started asking to try things. She began exploring, problem-solving, discovering what worked for her body.

Why We Built Sensory Galaxy

When we saw how much the SOS Approach helped Mackenzie, we couldn't stop thinking about one thing: How many other families are struggling like we were? How many parents feel like failures? How many kids are anxious at mealtimes? How many families don't even know that this framework exists?

We realized that the SOS Approach needed to be more accessible. It needed to be gamified so kids actually wanted to engage. It needed to transform a stressful mealtime into an adventure. It needed to celebrate every single phase—because looking at a new food IS a victory.

That's why we built Sensory Galaxy. It's rooted in the proven Sequential Oral Sensory Approach, but it brings it to life in a way that speaks to kids—with explorers, planets, badges, and real progress tracking that shows parents exactly what's happening.

For Every Family Like Ours

If you're reading this and your child struggles with selective eating—whether it's due to celiac disease, autism, sensory processing challenges, or something else entirely—know this: You're not alone. Your child isn't broken. And there's a way through this that doesn't involve battles or bribes or feeling like you're failing.

Mackenzie is thriving now. She still has her sensory preferences (and that's okay). But she's curious. She's adventurous. She feels safe exploring. And mealtimes are joyful again—because there's no pressure, just support.

That's what Sensory Galaxy is built for. For kids like Mackenzie. For families like ours. For the moment when you realize that the method matters more than the outcome, and that understanding your child's nervous system changes everything.
