🛡️ LLM Guardrails & Bridge Foods

10 Core Validation Rules

  1. Never recommend foods from allergen list
  2. Max 1 sensory difference per bridge food (texture XOR flavor XOR temperature XOR color)
  3. Confidence scoring: Only recommend if model confidence > 70%
  4. No foods logged <3 times before bridge recommendations activate
  5. Sensory distance calculation: Euclidean metric across 5-d sensory space
  6. Risk indicators: Flag low-confidence recommendations to parent
  7. Frequency caps: Max 2 new foods per week recommended
  8. No repetition: Never recommend same food twice within 30 days
  9. Age-appropriate: Remove choking hazards for ages <5
  10. Diversity enforcement: Ensure recommendations span ≥3 categories

Forbidden Combinations

  • Never pair sweet + bitter in same recommendation
  • Never recommend spicy to <6 year olds
  • Never suggest raw vegetables if child only eats cooked
  • Never recommend temperature jump >30°F in single bridge
  • Avoid color inversion (e.g., all green → all white in one step)

Bridge Food Activation Threshold

  • Requires: ≥3 unique foods logged with complete sensory profiles
  • Triggers: AI begins building sensory preference model after 3rd food
  • First recommendation: After 5 foods logged
  • Thereafter: 1 new recommendation per week (unless parent requests more)

🧠 Bridge Food Recommendation Algorithm (Phase 2-5 Core)

📖 COMPLETE ARCHITECTURE: See Bridge Food Algorithm v3 for the full specification with SOS exposure states, regression tracking, weight formulas, pseudo-code, and all Claude-validated fixes. This page shows the summary.

Why Rules-Based (Not LLM)

  • Transparency: Parents see exactly WHY we recommend each food (e.g., "same texture, similar flavor, warm temperature")
  • Cost: $0/month vs. $2,150/year for LLM. Phase 1 focus: prove product-market fit, not premium features
  • App Store Safety: Health apps must have explainability. Rules = defensible. LLM = "why did it suggest sushi to a kid with 4 foods?"
  • Control: Zero hallucinations. You own every recommendation. No surprises.

The Multi-Dimensional Sensory Model

Every food is rated on 6 sensory dimensions (0-10 scale):

  • Texture: liquid (0) → crunchy (8) → hard/brittle (10)
  • Flavor: 5 sub-dimensions (sweet, salty, savory, sour, bitter)
  • Temperature: cold (0) → hot (100°C)
  • Color: red, orange, yellow, green, brown, white, mixed
  • Mouthfeel: juicy, dry, creamy, crispy, tender, chewy
  • Preparation: raw, boiled, baked, fried, steamed

The Algorithm: From Safe Foods → Bridge Recommendations

  1. Onboarding Collects Safe Foods
    • Card #2: "What does your child eat?" (multi-select)
    • Card #2b: "Which 2-3 are they obsessed with?" (tag as Super Safe)
    • Weights: Super Safe 60% + Regular Safe 40% = baseline sensory profile
  2. Distance Calculation
    • For each of 100+ foods, calculate multi-dimensional distance from baseline
    • Formula: √[(texture_diff)² + (flavor_diff)² + (temp_diff)² + ... ] with weighted coefficients
    • Distance 0-2.5 = "Super Close", 2.5-4.5 = "Stretch", 4.5-6.5 = "Difficult", 6.5+ = "Too Far"
  3. Daily Recommendation Strategy (3 picks per day)
    • Safe Pick: Distance < 2.5. Role: "This is VERY close to what you like." High success.
    • Stretch Pick: Distance 2.5-4.5. Role: "Try this tiny challenge." One sensory dimension different.
    • Variety Pick: Targets nutritional gaps (needs more veggies? recommend colors/textures kid is avoiding)
  4. Novelty Control & Weekly Progression
    • Don't recommend same food twice in 7 days (prevent boredom)
    • Track weekly texture/color diversity, auto-shift recommendations (60% crunchy → 40% next week = gradual expansion)
    • Week 1: Safe picks. Week 2: Introduce stretch picks. Week 3+: Gradual sensory expansion

Handles Infinite Food Possibilities

  • Dimensionality: Kid with only 4 safe foods → algorithm calculates sensory baseline from those 4 → ranks all 100+ foods by similarity
  • Progressive Steps: Algorithm never jumps sensory distance. If kid likes warm salty crunchy, it recommends warm salty soft BEFORE jumping to cool bitter vegetables
  • Diversity Weighting: If kid has eaten crunchy foods 5x this week, algorithm weights soft/mixed textures higher next recommendations
  • Personalization: Each kid gets a unique recommendation order based on THEIR baseline, not generic "picky eaters eat these foods"

Example Recommendation Flow

Day 1 - Safe Pick: Seaweed snacks
"We picked this because: Crunchy like chicken nuggets, salty like you like it, but something new to try!"

Day 1 - Stretch Pick: Soft sweet potato fries
"Still warm like you like it, a little softer than usual, but pretty similar. Maybe try just one bite?"

Day 1 - Variety Pick: Yellow bell pepper slices  
"Your week has been brown & white foods. Let's add some color! This is sweet & crunchy like things you like."

Day 2 - Safe Pick: Crispy bacon
Distance: 1.8 (very close to baseline)
"Crunchy, salty, warm - all your favorite things with a fun new flavor!"

Week 2 - Shift Detected
Kid logged: mostly crunchy foods (7 of last 10 foods)
→ Algorithm increases soft texture weight by 30% for next week's recommendations
→ Still respects baseline (won't jump to something that's 90% different)

Implementation & Tuning

  • Phase 1: Build algorithm with default weights, test with beta families (week 1-2)
  • Phase 1 Results: Track which recommendations kids actually try (Safe vs. Stretch vs. Variety success rates)
  • Phase 1 Tuning: Adjust weights based on real data (e.g., if Stretch picks fail 80% of the time, widen distance gap)
  • Phase 2: If working well, lock in weights. Consider A/B testing variants later.
  • Never: Switch to LLM unless rules-based stops working (unlikely)
